Research Ethics Guidelines

1. INFORMED CONSENT
   - All participants must provide voluntary informed consent
   - Consent forms must be written in clear, understandable language
   - Participants have the right to withdraw at any time

2. CONFIDENTIALITY
   - Protect participant privacy and data security
   - Anonymize data where possible
   - Secure storage of sensitive information

3. AVOIDING HARM
   - Minimize risks to participants
   - Provide appropriate debriefing
   - Have support resources available if needed

4. SCIENTIFIC INTEGRITY
   - Report findings accurately and completely
   - Acknowledge limitations of the study
   - Avoid fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism

5. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
   - Disclose any potential conflicts
   - Maintain objectivity in research design and analysis
   - Separate personal interests from scientific conclusions

These guidelines should be reviewed and followed for all research involving human subjects.
