CHADO WINTER PREPARATION GUIDE
茶道 冬の準備
==============================

Season: Ro (炉) - Sunken Hearth Season
Period: November through April
Document Status: CURRENT

OVERVIEW
--------
The transition from Furo (風炉) to Ro marks one of the most significant 
seasonal changes in the practice of Chado. This guide outlines the 
necessary preparations for hosting a proper winter tea ceremony.

RO (SUNKEN HEARTH) SETUP
------------------------
1. The tatami mat containing the Ro must be inspected for wear
2. Ash bed (灰形 haigata) should be freshly prepared in the Tsukibai style
3. Charcoal arrangement follows the Shozumi pattern for initial heating
4. Kettle (釜 kama) selection: Recommend the Ashiya-gama for its heat retention

UTENSIL ROTATION
----------------
Winter utensils to bring from storage:
- Larger chawan (茶碗) with deeper bowls for warmth retention
- Heavier chakin (茶巾) for cooler temperatures
- Seasonal natsume (棗) with winter motifs (pine, plum, bamboo)

KAISEKI CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
Winter kaiseki should emphasize:
- Warming soups (汁物)
- Root vegetables (大根, 蕪, 牛蒡)
- Preserved foods appropriate to the season

HATSUGAMA (初釜) PREPARATION
---------------------------
The first tea ceremony of the new year requires special attention:
- Fresh matcha from the previous autumn's harvest
- Celebratory wagashi with auspicious designs
- All utensils thoroughly cleaned and inspected
